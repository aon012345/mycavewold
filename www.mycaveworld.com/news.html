On Hiatus: God's Personal Reasons
Hi everyone,

Our latest Korean novel is out now, and it's called On Hiatus: God's Personal Reasons! (The novel isn't on hiatus, that's just the name!) It's being translated by J.  Andie, who previously worked on Big Life.

Yu Il-Shin is a novel writer and a genius at that (as claimed by his editor-in-charge). But in this modern era where Hunters, monsters, and dungeons are rampant, he also hopes to awaken and gain some valuable abilities of his own and maybe have a successful change in career. He did awaken, and off he went to the Hunter Association to get himself examined. He was found to have awakened with Creation-type abilities and was evaluated to be of the lowest rank of all: G-rank.

Yet, something interesting happened to him. He chanced upon a strange game on his phone: God-Maker, which he hadn’t recalled installing before, but still decided to launch the mobile game anyway, trying to see what it was before uninstalling it altogether. 

In the game, he found a few ants crawling on the screen, and instinctively began crushing them. During this period of awakening and playing the strange mobile game, he was also working on a new novel that was put up for serialization on a famous webnovel site. 

If you are wondering whether he was killing the ants out of anger or not, and how he progressed on the game, check out On Hiatus: God’s Personal Reasons!

Cultivation Keys have been Implemented!
Hi everyone,

The update we announced on September 26th is live on our website! Cultivation Keys will now be earned from a daily check in instead of Regular Karma (automatically now, without needing to click it). One Cultivation Key = one chapter. And missions have replaced earning Regular Karma by commenting. For now, these missions only involve reading chapters.

For the app side of it, we're just about to submit an update to both Apple and Google, and that will hopefully be reviewed and released by them soon. Until then, it'll only be possible to earn Keys from our website.

We're looking forward to seeing how this goes, and the impact it has on the comment section! We'll do any necessary bug fixing over the next few days, and then beyond that we'll look at adding some mission variety.

If you encounter any problems, please feel free to share them in the comments here or through the usual channels:

  • Discord, pinging @lisyche, @Raido, and @Jaspaaar 
  • The customer support portal here
  • Our forum, in either the website feedback or app feedback subforum
In case anyone missed it, I'll copy and paste the mini FAQ section from the previous announcement:

What will happen to my Regular Karma?
After October 8th, new Regular Karma will no longer be distributed. It will still be possible to spend any remaining Regular Karma for now.

In the longer term (3-6 months from now), we plan to convert all remaining Regular Karma balances to Keys, at a conversion rate based on the average Karma price of a chapter. Converting it will avoid the technical baggage of us needing to continue to account for and work around having Regular Karma as an additional currency, both in our UI and on our backend.

Before we do the conversion, we will post an announcement a month in advance, also detailing the exact conversion rate.

Does this affect Golden Karma?
None of the details shared in this post will affect Golden Karma.

Does this affect the Wait to Unlock system?
Nope. Like Regular Karma, this system will be in addition to the free daily chapters from Wait to Unlock.

Which currency will be spent first?
When auto-unlock is enabled, Keys will be automatically spent before Regular Karma. When unlocking a chapter manually, you will be able to choose whether to spend a Key or Karma.

The Regular Karma System Will be Replaced on October 9th
Hi everyone,

On October 9th, we’re going to replace the daily free Regular Karma with something else: a free ticket system, where one ticket can be used to unlock one chapter, regardless of the chapter’s length. 

We’re doing this to simplify the current system, where the value of Karma is somewhat nebulous, especially for new/prospective readers. 

We want to be explicit on this: The number of tickets able to be gained each day will be at least equal to the number of average-length chapters able to be unlocked by the daily earnable amount of Regular Karma. More straightforwardly: this won't reduce the number of chapters you're able to unlock for free, and that is not the intention of this change.

The first source of tickets will be a daily check in, replacing the Regular Karma check in. And the longer your daily streak becomes, the more tickets you will receive from each check in.

Additionally, this is where we’re finally going to replace the system of earning Regular Karma by posting comments. The original intention of it was a positive one: to encourage and reward community participation. But it was accompanied by endless spam comments, and despite our efforts to constantly remove spam and penalise accounts, it still degraded the experience of the comment section. 

So, the second source of tickets will be a missions system, equivalent to the Regular Karma which needed to be proactively earned from commenting. These missions will change over time. You might receive tickets for reading a certain number of chapters, writing a review, or upvoting a certain number of comments for example. We’re also going to look at implementing missions of viewing ads to receive tickets (this would be in addition to other missions. Not instead of).

Here’s a sample of what it will look like:

There’s one key detail missing though: we haven’t settled on a name for this new ticket currency yet! We’ve shortlisted a few ideas, but we’d like to get your input. So, we’ve made a survey, and the link is here! 

The survey will stay open until September 29th, and then we’ll implement the chosen name ready for October 9th. For fun, the survey results will be publicly visible. The vote isn’t binding though; we might decide to select the name with the second most votes, or, if there’s a suggestion in the Other field that we really like, we might ditch the shortlist and go with that instead!

The main naming issue we’re weighing up is descriptive names vs creative names. With descriptive names (plain ones like ‘chapter ticket’), it’s easier to understand that 1 ticket = 1 chapter. But creative names are more fun.

I’ll answer a few expected questions now.

What will happen to my Regular Karma?
After October 8th, new Regular Karma will no longer be distributed. It will still be possible to spend any remaining Regular Karma for now.

In the longer term (3-6 months from now), we plan to convert all remaining Regular Karma balances to tickets, at a conversion rate based on the average Karma price of a chapter. Converting it will avoid the technical baggage of us needing to continue to account for and work around having Regular Karma as an additional currency, both in our UI and on our backend.

Before we do the conversion, we will post an announcement a month in advance, also detailing the exact conversion rate.

Does this affect Golden Karma?
None of the details shared in this post will affect Golden Karma.

Does this affect the Wait to Unlock system?
Nope. Like Regular Karma, this system will be in addition to the free daily chapters from Wait to Unlock.

Which currency will be spent first?
When auto-unlock is enabled, tickets will be automatically spent before Regular Karma. When unlocking a chapter manually, you will be able to choose whether to spend a ticket or Karma. 

If you have any other questions, feel free to post a comment! With this implemented, sorting the comment section by New should become more usable, which I’m definitely looking forward to. (And if it doesn’t fix it, then we’ll have to look at outright banning thanks comments). Plus the missions will give us more flexibility for running events and providing variety in earning tickets!
